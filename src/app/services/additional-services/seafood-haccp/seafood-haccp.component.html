Seafood HACCP and the FDA

The Food and Drug Administration (FDA) final regulations to ensure the safe and sanitary processing of fish and fishery products mandate the application of Hazard Analysis Critical Control Point (HACCP) principles to the processing of seafood. HACCP is a preventive system of hazard control that can be used by processors to ensure the safety of their products to consumers. See the full FDA Final rule regarding Seafood and HACCP here.
